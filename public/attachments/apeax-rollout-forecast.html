Commercial Discussion

GrafShield (Anti-Graffiti Film) — ASI Wants APEAX Quality

APEAX meeting — 2 April 2026. ASI wants to use APEAX film for GrafShield. The only barrier is material cost.

The Situation

ASI's GrafShield product is anti-graffiti sacrificial film applied to bus sideglass interiors — 6 panes per bus. Our installation team has used APEAX Gloss 60 on-site and the product is excellent. The clarity and optical quality of APEAX film is noticeably superior to our current local supplier's product. We WANT to use APEAX for GrafShield. The only barrier is material cost.

The Problem: Material Cost Gap

What ASI Needs from APEAX

We want APEAX-grade clarity and protection performance at a price point that makes GrafShield commercially competitive against local sourcing. The local product has inferior optical quality — if APEAX can close the cost gap, we switch immediately.

Option 1 (Preferred): Volume Discount on Gloss 60 for Anti-Graffiti

ASI needs material cost at or below $90/pane landed to achieve workable margin economics. At 305 buses × 6 panes = 1,830 panes/year, that's significant volume. A GrafShield-specific T1 discount or volume rebate would close the gap.

Option 2: Defender Gloss 60 at T1 ($449.40 USD/roll)

Lower-cost film that still delivers APEAX optical clarity. At $449.40 T1 vs $569.40 for Xtreme, material cost drops to ~$95/pane landed (sea). Close to target. 5-year warranty vs 10-year — acceptable for a sacrificial anti-graffiti application that gets replaced on damage anyway.

Option 3: Dedicated Anti-Graffiti SKU

If APEAX has or can develop a sacrificial-grade film optimised for anti-graffiti (doesn't need 10-year PPF durability, does need optical clarity), this could be the ideal solution. ASI would be the anchor customer for the Australian bus market.
